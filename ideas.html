ThinkTank Draft: Feature extraction

July 2022

I am currently trying to understand feature extraction for kmeans clustering of images. I am using for example 32 gabor filters - this means every image (64x64) gets 32 filtered versions. Should I:

  1. generate a feature vector (646432=131072) by just concatenating those?
  2. generate a feature vector (64*64=4096) by somehow combining the pixels?
  3. generate one feature for each filter (=32)?
  4. do something else?
  • V: Make histograms per filter (32 filters x 10 bins = 320 features). This way images don't need to be the same size. Use adaptive binning for the bins.
  • S: In any case, after applying Gabor filters to the image you'll want to look into methods to combine them (not just concatenating) such as Local Energy, Mean Amplitude, Phase Amplitude... your feature vectors will have to be low-dimensional (e.g., 1x32).

ThinkTank Draft: Saliency Maps

July 2022

Any suggestions how to do saliency maps for binary (one output neuron 0/1)? - it works for class 1, but not for class 0.

  • B: As for the problem of creating saliency maps directly from the BCE setup, I have a strong intuition that there must be a '1-x' trick somewhere in the pipeline that will convert the saliency maps of one class to zero class. But I am not sure where exactly in the pipeline. It's an interesting mathematical exercise though, if you would like to try.
  • D: When applying attribution to binary classification, the attribution scores are the same (but opposite) for both classes. This is because if a pixel is very important for determining the image is class 0, it must also be very important for determining the image is not class 1. Put another way, a pixel is either important for the 1:0 classification, or it isn't. You can get the attribution map for the other class in binary classification just by taking the negative* of the first class. (*requires an attribution method with a typical mathematical output of course)

ThinkTank Draft: The problems with "green" energy.

June 2022

  • Natural gas emissions: Methane is the second biggest contributor to global warming. Source: International Energy Agency
  • Hydroelectric dam emissions: Hydroelectric dams generate a surprising amount of greenhouse gas emissions. Source: The Guardian, English
  • Solar panel recycling: Millions of solar panels have been installed in the last two decades—and since they typically last between 25 and 30 years, many will soon be ready for retirement and probably headed to a landfill. Recycling the devices is currently much more expensive than just discarding them. Source: MIT Tech Review, English
  • Wind turbine recycling: turbine blades are currently considered non-recycable due to their composite material: fibreglass (fiber-reinforced plastic using glass fiber). This is why discarded wind turbine blades often end up being buried in the ground. Source: Futurezone, German
